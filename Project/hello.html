Regional Analysis

Northern Region

The Northern Plains are characterized by intensive agriculture due to fertile alluvial soil and extensive irrigation facilities.

  • Land Holding: Average size of 3.7 ha
  • Irrigation: Primarily canal and tube well irrigation
  • Cropping: Wheat, Rice, Sugarcane
  • Well Depth: Average depth of 83 meters

Southern Region

The Southern Peninsula relies on well and tank irrigation, with a mix of commercial and subsistence farming.

  • Land Holding: Average size of 1.9 ha
  • Irrigation: Primarily well and tank irrigation
  • Cropping: Rice, Coconut, Spices
  • Well Depth: Average depth of 67 meters

Eastern Region

The Eastern Region is characterized by high rainfall and river systems, supporting rice and jute cultivation.

  • Land Holding: Average size of 1.2 ha
  • Irrigation: Primarily rainwater and river irrigation
  • Cropping: Rice, Jute, Tea
  • Well Depth: Average depth of 45 meters

Western Region

The Western Region faces water scarcity and relies on tube well irrigation for crops like cotton and groundnut.

  • Land Holding: Average size of 2.8 ha
  • Irrigation: Primarily tube well irrigation
  • Cropping: Cotton, Groundnut, Bajra
  • Well Depth: Average depth of 125 meters

Central Region

The Central Region has a mix of rainfed and irrigated agriculture, with soybean and pulses as major crops.

  • Land Holding: Average size of 2.4 ha
  • Irrigation: Primarily well and tank irrigation
  • Cropping: Soybean, Pulses, Wheat
  • Well Depth: Average depth of 90 meters
